Attack Attack!



Attack Attack! was an Ohio band formed in 2006 when Johnny Franck, Andrew Whiting, Nick White and Andrew Wetzel met Austin Carlile while playing in local high school bands. After gaining some underground attention off the release of their EP If Guns Are Outlawed, Can We Use Swords?, Attack Attack! would go on to redefine the post hardcore genre, signing to Rise Records and releasing their first studio album Someday Came Suddenly

The band went through multiple lineup changes in 2009 over issues the rest of the band had with frontman Austin Carlile. Caleb Shomo took over as the frontman for the bands next studio album, which was self titled.

The band was plagued by more lineup changes with the departure of singer Johnny Franck shortly after the release of their self titled album. This resulted Caleb taking on both singing and screaming parts for the bands next release, This Means War in 2012.
